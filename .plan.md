# Implementation Plan: Convert Opponent Hand from Fan to Rail

## Summary

Currently, opponent hands (`handHidden: true`) render as a `HiddenHandFan` component — a spread of face-down card backs in a rotated arc with a count badge. The local player's hand (`handHidden: false`) renders as a horizontal rail of face-up cards. The goal is to make opponent hands render as a rail of face-down card backs, matching the rail layout, and remove the fan component entirely.

## Key Insight

The `handHidden` flag serves **two** purposes:
1. **Display mode**: Fan vs Rail layout (what we're changing)
2. **Card visibility**: Whether to show card faces or card backs (must keep)

We need to decouple these: all hands should use the rail layout, but opponent cards in the HAND zone should still show as card backs.

## Edge Cases and Considerations

1. **Empty opponent hand**: When `hand.length === 0` and no command/exile/graveyard cards exist, `hasRailContent` will be false and "No cards ready" is displayed — same behavior as before.

2. **Commander cards for hidden hands**: Previously shown as a "featured" card overlapping the fan. Now they appear inline in the rail as a face-up `CardView` (since they're in `Zone.COMMAND`, not `Zone.HAND`, the card-back branch in `renderRailCard` won't trigger). This is correct — commanders are public information.

3. **Playable exile/graveyard cards for hidden hands**: These were already rendered in the rail (they were not filtered out by the `Zone.HAND` filter). No change in behavior.

4. **Hover effects on card backs**: The card-back div rendered for hidden HAND cards does not wire up `onMouseEnter` for hover presentation effects (scale/lift). This is intentional — there's no interaction with face-down opponent cards. Note: if face-up cards (commanders, playable exile cards) are in the same rail as card backs, hovering over them will still trigger the neighbor-lift effect via `getHandPresentation`, but card backs won't respond visually since they don't consume `presentation`. This is acceptable behavior — opponents' rails are not interactive.

5. **Card sizing**: The card-back divs use `data-variant="hand"` which applies the standard `--card-width` and `--card-height` CSS variables and the negative `margin-right` overlap. This ensures they're sized and spaced identically to face-up rail cards.

6. **Drag and drop**: Card backs don't support drag — this is correct since opponents' hidden hand cards have no legal actions for the local player.

7. **Hand card count**: The fan previously showed a count badge. After this change, the count is implicitly visible since each card back is rendered individually in the rail. If a count indicator is desired, it could be added later, but it's not necessary since the individual cards serve as a visual count.

8. **Animation continuity**: Hidden hand cards don't register with `registerCardElement`, which is correct. The animation system in `GameBoard.tsx` falls back to the zone anchor (`HAND` area div registered at line 542) when individual card nodes aren't found (line 172–173). The `hiddenSource` flag (line 188) correctly marks these animations as originating from a hidden hand.
